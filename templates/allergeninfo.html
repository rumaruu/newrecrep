Gluten Allergy/Intolerance (Celiac Disease)

Description: An immune response triggered by the ingestion of gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye.

Symptoms: Digestive issues (abdominal pain, bloating, diarrhea), fatigue, skin rashes.

Substitutes: Gluten-free grains like rice, quinoa, and buckwheat, gluten-free flours (almond flour, coconut flour), and gluten-free products.



Dairy Allergy/Lactose Intolerance

Description: Allergy: An immune response to proteins in cow's milk. Intolerance: Inability to digest lactose, the sugar in milk.

Symptoms: Digestive discomfort (bloating, gas, diarrhea), skin rashes, respiratory problems.

Substitutes: Plant-based milk alternatives (almond milk, soy milk, oat milk), dairy-free cheeses, and yogurt made from coconut, almond, or soy.


Nut Allergy

Description: An immune reaction to proteins found in certain nuts, such as peanuts, tree nuts (almonds, cashews, walnuts), or both.

Symptoms: Skin reactions (rashes, itching), difficulty breathing, digestive issues.

Substitutes: Seeds (sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds), seed butter (sunflower seed butter, pumpkin seed butter), and non-nut flours (coconut flour, chickpea flour).


Shellfish Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to shellfish, including crustaceans (shrimp, lobster, crab) and mollusks (clams, mussels, oysters).

Symptoms: Swelling, hives, difficulty breathing, digestive discomfort.

Substitutes: Fish, poultry, or vegetarian options for seafood flavors, such as seaweed or kelp-based products.


Egg Allergy

Description: An immune response to proteins found in eggs, particularly the proteins in egg whites.

Symptoms: Skin reactions (hives, itching), digestive issues, respiratory problems.

Substitutes: Commercial egg replacements, such as applesauce, mashed bananas, silken tofu, or flaxseed meal mixed with water.


Soy Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to proteins found in soybeans and soy-based products.

Symptoms: Digestive issues, skin reactions (hives, itching), respiratory problems.

Substitutes: Almond milk, coconut aminos (instead of soy sauce), chickpea miso paste (instead of soy-based miso), and alternative protein sources like legumes, quinoa, or hemp seeds.


Fish Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to proteins found in various types of fish.

Symptoms: Swelling, hives, difficulty breathing, digestive discomfort.

Substitutes: Poultry, beef, pork, or vegetarian options for seafood flavors, such as seaweed or kelp-based products.


Sesame Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to proteins found in sesame seeds and sesame-based products.

Symptoms: Skin reactions (rashes, hives), digestive issues, respiratory problems.

Substitutes: Sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, or flaxseeds as toppings, and alternative oils like olive oil or coconut oil for cooking.


Corn Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to proteins found in corn or a sensitivity to corn-related components.

Symptoms: Digestive issues, skin reactions, respiratory problems.

Substitutes: Rice, quinoa, or gluten-free grains as alternatives, arrowroot starch or tapioca starch instead of cornstarch, and alternative sweeteners like maple syrup or honey.


Wheat Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to proteins found in wheat.

Symptoms: Digestive issues, skin reactions (rashes, hives), respiratory problems.

Substitutes: Gluten-free flours (rice flour, tapioca flour, sorghum flour), oats (if certified gluten-free), and alternative grains like amaranth or millet.


Peanut Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to proteins found in peanuts.

Symptoms: Swelling, hives, difficulty breathing, digestive discomfort.

Substitutes: Other nut butters like almond butter or sunflower seed butter, or alternative spreads made from pumpkin seeds, hemp seeds, or soybeans.


Sulfite Sensitivity

Description: Sensitivity to sulfites, which are compounds used as preservatives in certain foods and beverages.

Symptoms: Asthma-like symptoms, headache, digestive issues.

Substitutes: Fresh fruits and vegetables instead of dried or processed ones, and vinegar or lemon juice as a substitute for sulfite-containing condiments like ketchup or salad dressings.


Nightshade Allergy

Description: An allergic reaction to plants in the nightshade family, such as tomatoes, potatoes, peppers, and eggplants.

Symptoms: Digestive issues, skin reactions, joint pain.

Substitutes: Alternatives to nightshade vegetables like sweet potatoes instead of white potatoes, and spices like turmeric or ginger as substitutes for paprika or chili powder.


Shellfish Sensitivity

Description: A sensitivity or allergic reaction to shellfish, including crustaceans and mollusks.

Symptoms: Digestive discomfort, skin reactions, respiratory issues.

Substitutes: Fish, poultry, or vegetarian options for seafood flavors, such as seaweed or kelp-based products.


Histamine Intolerance

Description: Difficulty in breaking down histamine, a compound found in various foods and beverages, leading to an intolerance.

Symptoms: Headaches, flushing, hives, digestive issues.

Substitutes: Freshly cooked foods instead of aged or fermented ones, low-histamine foods like fresh fruits and vegetables, and alternative seasonings like lemon juice or fresh herbs.
